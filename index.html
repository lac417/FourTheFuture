Who Am I?

I am unregistered.

Help me become documented.

According to the UN, one in four children under the age of five worldwide do not have a claim over their digital identity, because their birth was not registered. This causes them to be excluded from employment and education opportunities, as well as government services later on in life.

Without legal proof of identity, children are left uncounted and invisible.

For many countries, birth registration is taken for granted as the norm following childbirth. But in too many others, it is a critical step missing to establish a childs legal proof of identity. According to UNICEF, children are invisible to their governments without registration; they could miss out on their rights being protected and upheld, as well as essential services like health care and education.


You can help me.

Any donation that can be made, helps.

According to UNICEF, women do not have the same rights or ability to register their childs birth as men in many parts of the world. There are still 25 countries where women do not have the same rights as men to legally pass their nationality to their own children. This kind of gender discrimination in national laws and policies needs to be reviewed and revised to eliminate the negative impacts on communities.
